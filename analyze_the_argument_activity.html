Written Activity: Analyze the Argument

Critical Thinking & Informal Fallacies
Directions: Read each argument carefully. Determine the informal fallacy committed, then explain in 2–3 sentences why the reasoning is faulty.
Time Elapsed: 00:00
1. Analyze the following scenario:
A school advertisement says, "Our students are outstanding." After enrolling, Carlo complains because he expected every student to have excellent grades. The school explains that "outstanding" meant students who actively participate in extracurricular activities.
2. Analyze the following scenario:
A basketball team has five excellent players. Therefore, the team will certainly become the champion.
3. Analyze the following scenario:
The school has an excellent Science Department. Therefore, every Science teacher in the school must be excellent.
4. Analyze the following scenario:
The teacher announced, "Students who finish the test early may leave." Some students interpreted this to mean that they could leave without submitting their papers.
5. Analyze the following scenario:
A poster says, "NO STUDENTS EAT FREE." Some people interpret it as students are not allowed to eat for free, while others understand it as students should not eat without paying.
6. Analyze the following scenario:
"No one has ever proven that extraterrestrial life does not exist. Therefore, aliens definitely exist."
7. Analyze the following scenario:
"Please pass me in this subject. If I fail, my parents will be very disappointed, and I might lose my scholarship."
8. Analyze the following scenario:
"We should ignore Marco's proposal for improving the school canteen because he failed Mathematics last semester."
9. Analyze the following scenario:
"If you don't support my proposal, I'll make sure you don't become the leader of our group."
10. Analyze the following scenario:
"A famous actor says this vitamin supplement improves memory. Therefore, it must really work."
